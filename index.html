Projects

1. Federated Learning Mini Project

My mini-project focuses on developing defense mechanisms against poisoning attacks in federated learning. In federated learning, multiple participants train a shared model without exposing their local data. However, malicious participants can perform poisoning attacks by injecting harmful updates, compromising the model’s performance or causing biased outputs. This project explores techniques like robust aggregation methods and anomaly detection to identify and mitigate such attacks, ensuring the integrity and reliability of the global model.

2. Predicting Future Poverty Levels Using Socioeconomic Indicators.

As part of my IBM CSRBox SkillsBuild virtual internship, I worked on a project titled "Predicting Future Poverty Levels Using Socioeconomic Indicators." This project aimed to develop predictive models using historical socioeconomic data to anticipate poverty trends and propose actionable solutions aligned with Sustainable Development Goal 1 (No Poverty). Through the use of Python, Scikit-learn, and Tableau, I analyzed key indicators such as GDP, unemployment, and access to education to derive insights that can support data-driven policy-making and poverty alleviation strategies.
